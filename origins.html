Origins

Initial Idea

Team K’s decision to build a solution that eliminates the lack of focus many students tend to have was not the initial idea.

On the first day our group met to discuss information problems, there was one problem in particular we all seemed to have; finding the ideal place to study on campus. The University of Washington has many libraries, study rooms and cafés all with different environments; the process of searching for the perfect spot that meets specific study needs can be an overwhelming process.

We each interviewed two students about this problem. We asked how they go about finding a study spot and what kind of conditions are required for a successful study session. After comparing information with my group members, we discovered several shortcomings with this problem:

  1. This was not as big of an issue as we thought it was; some claimed to have no problem with finding a specific study spot.
  2. Several students found that libraries and cafes did not match their ideal study environment
  3. The study needs change based on what type of studying is being done, meaning an individual does not need just one study spot.
  4. SpaceScout is a program that students already use and provides a solution for our problem (such as a survey of study conditions, a map and pictures of each study location, current available space)

At this point, we realized that finding the ideal study location was not the actual problem students were having. Thinking about it now, I think we decided on the problem based on a specific solution in mind. We were told not to think about solutions at this point, but we did anyways and this stole time from us. Using the information we had at this point, we took a step back. Finding the ideal study spot was meant to optimize focus and limit distractions; this led us to realize the main issue: students have a hard time focusing on schoolwork because there are too many distractions.

Although I felt like it was too late to start over, my groupmates and I agreed that it would be worth the time to pursue this problem space.

We feel this is a major source for lack of focus because the majority of schoolwork requires the use of a computer which is a main portal to distractions.
